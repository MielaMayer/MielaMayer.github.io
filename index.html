Climate Scientist and investor at Third Sphere, focused on early stage climate tech. Building out Cap Stack Compass to be an open collaborative tool that raises the floor on non-dilutive funding access. ✦Writing & Work

U.S. Cities' Renewable Energy Targets: Rhetoric or Reality?

Yale Environmental Studies senior thesis · 2019

An analysis of the renewable-energy commitments of 61 "active" and 18 "achieved" U.S. cities — how cities actually buy electricity, a demand forecast by city, region, and utility type, and whether ambitious municipal targets can realistically be met.

Questioning our Yield Gap: Agroforestry's Potential within American Agriculture

Essay · Environmental research

Argues that the perceived "yield gap" of regenerative methods is mismeasured, and that agroforestry and perennial polycultures can close it while sequestering carbon and building resilience across the American agriculture industry. A follow-on to Learning to Let Nature Do the Work.

Learning to Let Nature Do the Work

A Review of Permaculture's Potential within American Agriculture · Essay

An introduction to permaculture as a design system — sheet mulching, hugelkultur and keyline farming, perennial polycultures and guilds — and a case for working with natural succession rather than against it.

Analyzing Planktonic Foraminifera as a Proxy for Past Sea Surface Temperature

Yale senior project · Paleoclimate · with Pincelli Hull & Robin Canavan

A statistical analysis testing whether bulk, mixed-species foraminifera samples can yield reliable clumped-isotope (∆₄₇) sea-surface temperatures — making paleoclimate reconstruction cheaper and more replicable.
